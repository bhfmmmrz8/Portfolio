About

I am a fun-loving Brooklyn based art-enthusiast with a focus in graphic design. I have a keen interest in type and language based art and activism or socially engaged design work which has become a part of my primary design practice. I recently acquired my B.A degree in Electronic Design and Multimedia from CCNY Art Dept and was also a recipient of the James R. Steers Prize in Art. I have recently completed a summer internship with Esther K. Smith at Purgatory Pie Press , where I assisted in various bookbinding projects. More recently I designed the main visuals for BMCC's Women's HerStory Month, which were used form events and conferences. I also had the honor to present a solo art exhibiton called Intricacies which was sponsored by BMCC's Women's Resource Center.

I previously acquired an Associate in Multimedia Arts and Communications from Borough of Manhattan Community College, and currently work as a Supplemental Instructor at the Learning Resource Center. My most enjoyable design aspects are hand drawn typography, vector illustration, logo/branding and publishing.

When I’m not designing, I invest my time in photography, henna, painting, calligraphy and bookbinding. I also run an Etsy shop, RubysCornerltd, where I sell handmade craft as a side gig.

Naqsh Henna Cafe

Naqsh (or Lovely Humans) is a branding project for a cafe design using the art of henna and its traditional roots and values to empower women of Pakistan and to bring them and other marginalized communities in Lahore more employment opportunities. Like other cafés in Pakistan, the Henna Café will educate the underprivileged communities in the traditional art of henna, creating new economic opportunities and a sense of inclusion.

To avoid controversy in the patriarchal environment, this message is communicated subtly in the brand identity by integrating the geometric and vegetal patterns inherent to henna art with a decorative fusion of Latin alphabet and Urdu lettering. In addition, the brand will instate the means of repurposing materials with underlying impressions of rethinking life and personal choices.

Promoting equality and inclusion, the café will also serve as a ‘safe haven’ for those affected by social degrading and blind hate. The deliverables will include a Naqsh Style Guide, wallpaper patterns, business and loyalty cards, work apparel, nametags, and café utilities such as coasters, cups, sleeves, and napkins. My collaterals will include food labels, pastry boxes, and wrappings. In addition, I will produce audio for background music, which will consist of poems by Naheed. All of these will incorporate the concept of repurposing materials as DIY projects.

  • Client: B.A. Design Thesis : EDM CCNY
  • Software: Adobe Illustrator, InDesign Photoshop, Screenprinting, Laser cutting
  • Service: Branding, Graphic Design, Layout & Typography
  • Purpose: Social engagement, activism, women empowerment

Portfolio Book

For the final in portfolio class, I created a print portfolio which consists of a custom design and layout. I ustilised my knowledge of book making and screenprinting to create every bit that makes up this book.

The book uses assets from my personal brand identity to create a recurrent presence.

The interval pages were screenprinted on alternating colored cardstock pages. The context pages were printed and mounted on vellum paper to make them sturdy. While the outside cover is crafted with heavy book board coverd in book cloth.

The front cover has an embossed logo which was achieved by mounting a lasercut of my personal logo. The closure for the book was done through magnet clips.

  • Techniques: Screen Printing, Bookbinding
  • Software + Medium: Adobe InDesign, Screenprinting, Bookbinding
